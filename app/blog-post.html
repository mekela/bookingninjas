Booking Ninjas Improve Hotel Management

David Harroch 08 Nov 2019 10 views

Stop hiring assholes, and 11 other tips to save your hotel’s soul
Or
12 reasons you suck at hotel management

While our guests visit hotels to relax, it takes a lot of hard work to make comfort look so good. Isn't it better to work smarter, not harder? Let's examine these top 12 tips to increase the efficiency of managing your hotel and employees.

Effective communication

Make sure employees and management use a reliable communication channel like Slack, Microsoft Teams, or even WhatsApp. Although relatively new, these workplace communication tools are popular because of their ease-of-use and resulting productivity. We don't send smoke signals anymore; use the tools of today to achieve the smart hotel management of tomorrow.

Cut the clutter

We are oversubscribed and deluged by email. Reduce the clutter, unsubscribe liberally, and focus only on hospitality industry news and publications.

Hire experts

As much as we may want to, you can't do it all. Outsource vital business responsibilities like marketing, accounting, and other professional services that don't necessarily require in-house employees. Another hidden plus of hiring contractors or going with external talent is potentially saving money on full-time employee benefits.

Promote guest reviews on-site

By encouraging guest feedback in-room or with signage in the lobby, your marketing team can reduce time spent doing follow-up emails and chasing down guests for reviews. Also, by capturing guest response while on-site, you can capitalize on your guests' recency bias while they are still enjoying their visit.

Answer guest questions before they ask them

A short, one-page FAQ or hotel overview left in-room can go a long way to alleviating more mundane, frequently asked questions of the front desk. Free your hotel staff from the tyranny of questions about Wi-Fi passwords, breakfast hours, and more so they can be more productive with tasks worthy of their talents.

Make a social media calendar

…and stick to it. Creating and maintaining an informative but entertaining content calendar across major social media platforms is the cheap, basic marketing of our digital age. There are numerous professional social media management applications like SproutSocial or Recurpost to automate regular posting for you.

Hire team players

The hotel business can be unpredictable, with the constant turnover of guests and events. Make sure you hire someone with the flexibility to cover the odd extra or weekend shift, pitch in outside their normal job responsibilities when needed, and be willing to learn the basics of multiple jobs in the hotel to see how the whole workplace ecosystem connects.

Think global but hire local

There's an added bonus in hiring employees who are familiar with the area and can offer guests their local perspective and expertise.

Hire the right people

Yes, it's a cliché, but we need to hire candidates with universal hospitality characteristics of friendliness, positive attitude, and a willingness to serve. Both employees and guests will be willing to work with new team members if they are pleasant to be around and exhibit an agreeable demeanor reflective of the hotel's brand.

Use cloud-based property management

No need to stay chained behind your desk to get stuff done. By using cloud-based, mobile-friendly, professional property management software, you will increase personal productivity and your accessibility to employees.

Train 'em up

Have your front desk employees well-trained on the hotel property management system (PMS). It is a powerful tool that covers bookings, guest personas, and online reservations. By mastering its features and automations, your staff can be more efficient with their time.

Use the PMS to maximize your efficiency

Use technology designed for the hotel industry so you can take advantage of industry-specific data and innovation. With the advances in artificial intelligence, machine learning, reporting, and analytical insights, there has never been a better time to be a hotel executive. The future of this industry is digital; expect nothing less from your hotel management system.
