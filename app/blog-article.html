What does pms stand for in hotels?
1988 10 march 2019

The rapid advancement of technology have shifted the mindset of our generation. The sudden ease of how seemingly complex activities can be done by the click of a button have made people increase their standards of satisfaction from the clients end and the staff end.

That uprising, aptly termed the digital revolution, has found its way to the hospitality industry, mainly hotels. Activities are now managed digitally, user experience improved, and processes runs faster, compared to the manual times. The most noticeable of these systems is the PMS, fully called the Property Management System.

They are systems used by hotels to handle the management of all properties under the hotel. They have recently been employed by hotels to manage their properties in a more efficient way. A benefit of the PMS is that they are faster and more efficient than the old paper way. They assist in improving customer relations and generally handle administrative tasks. The best part is that recently developed PMS are web and cloud based, meaning the hotel can be monitored and managed from virtually anywhere in the world.

A PMS, however should not be confused with a Hotel Management System, or HMS. An HMS integrates all the other systems together. It is the overall technology that helps hotel administrators carry out tasks and perform external operations like handling bookings. It helps integrates your booking system, and assists you in improving customer relations.

Another system worth mentioning is the Channel Manager. It is a system that enables hotels to set room rates and availability, and sends it to all the distribution channels. It's a faster way of updating rates as it is done from a single system to update all other platforms.

These advancements and more have been applied to the hospitality industry and is recommended for business use, so as to improve efficiency, reduce costs, improve customer relations and increase revenue. It's time to take a look into them, and consider applying it to our businesses.
