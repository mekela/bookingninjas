What is a property management system?

Are you a property manager, business owner, or hotelier? A property management software can make your life easier. A property manager software (PMS) is a software application built for hospitality accommodations, commercial residential rental properties, and other buyers who manage a type of property business (for example, self storage areas and parking garages).

The history of Property Management Systems

A PMS is a centralized system to organize, schedule, and perform the day-to-day functions and transactions involved in rental and hospitality businesses. This means automating tasks like:

1970’s

The beginning of the Industrial Revolution, which facilitated the construction of hotels in mainland Europe, England, and America.While inns and family-style management were popular, modern innovations began cropping up. The Tremont hotel in Boston was the first with an ‘a-la-carte’ menu and indoor toilets, and The Holt House in New York City was the first to offer a lift for luggage.

1970’s

The beginning of construction of hotels for business people. Several factors sparked the movement, such as:

  • Airlines willing to extend their efforts into the hotel domains.
  • The prosperity in the Middle East, attracting business people all over the world. This initiated the development of hotels primarily for business people in Middle Eastern cities like Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Riyadh, and Jeddah. This kicked off the development of international hotels.
1980’s

The first administrative hotel management systems appeared on the market, offering greater independence from human resources. The hotel industry became more competitive and business travelers and retirees became important targets.

1990s-2001

The dotcom boom was in full effect. Over-the-Air programming meant that websites were more accessible and better quality. Operational Data Stores enabled the integration of data from multiple spaces.

2002-2018

Google and startup culture flourished, with plenty of new entrants to the property management software space like Cloudbeds and Mews. Web experiences became accessible on multiple devices and platforms. Metasearch enabled business discoverability, and travel sites like Orbitz surged in popularity.
